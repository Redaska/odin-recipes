Moroccan Couscous

Description

Moroccan cuisine is a fusion of Mediterranean, Arab, and African flavors, featuring spices, herbs, vegetables, and meats. Popular dishes include tagines, couscous, and harira soup, often accompanied by mint tea and pastries.

Ingredients

  • Couscous grains
  • Water or broth
  • Salt
  • Olive oil or butter
  • Vegetables, such as carrots, onions, and bell peppers
  • Meat, such as chicken or lamb
  • Spices, such as cumin, paprika, turmeric, and cinnamon
  • Dried fruit, such as raisins or apricots (optional)
  • Fresh herbs, such as parsley or cilantro (optional)

Steps

  1. Bring water or broth to a boil in a pot, and add salt and a drizzle of olive oil or butter.
  2. Add couscous grains to the pot, stirring to ensure they are fully submerged in the liquid.
  3. Turn off the heat, cover the pot, and let the couscous sit for 5-10 minutes, or until it has absorbed all the liquid and become tender.
  4. Fluff the couscous with a fork to break up any clumps.
  5. While the couscous is cooking, prepare any vegetables or meats you wish to add to the dish. This may involve saut√©ing or roasting vegetables, or cooking meat in a separate pan.
  6. Once the couscous is cooked and fluffed, add in any cooked vegetables or meats, along with spices and herbs to taste.
  7. Stir everything together until well combined, and serve hot.
